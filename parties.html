UK Political Parties

An overview of the main political parties across the United Kingdom, including parties from Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

EnglandScotlandWalesNorthern Ireland

England

Labour

Leader: Keir Starmer

Position: Centre-left

Founded: 1900

A centre-left party focused on public services, workers' rights, and social equality. Founded in 1900 by trade unions and socialist societies.

Key priorities

  • Economic growth and stability
  • NHS investment and reform
  • Green energy transition
  • Workers' rights and fair pay
  • Housing and infrastructure

Conservative

Leader: Kemi Badenoch

Position: Centre-right

Founded: 1834

A centre-right party emphasising lower taxes, free markets, and national sovereignty. The oldest political party in the UK, founded in 1834.

Key priorities

  • Lower taxes and deregulation
  • Strong national defence
  • Controlled immigration
  • Brexit opportunities
  • Education standards

Liberal Democrat

Leader: Ed Davey

Position: Centrist

Founded: 1988

A centrist party supporting civil liberties, electoral reform, and environmental protection. Formed in 1988 from the Liberal-SDP merger.

Key priorities

  • Electoral reform (proportional representation)
  • Climate action
  • Investment in health and social care
  • Civil liberties protections
  • Free childcare and education

Green Party

Leader: Carla Denyer & Adrian Ramsay

Position: Left-wing

Founded: 1990

A left-wing party prioritising climate action, social justice, and grassroots democracy. Campaigns for an environmentally sustainable economy.

Key priorities

  • Net-zero carbon emissions by 2030
  • Public ownership of key services
  • Universal basic income
  • Housing as a human right
  • Renewable energy investment

Reform UK

Leader: Nigel Farage

Position: Right-wing populist

Founded: 2018

A right-wing populist party supporting lower immigration, tax cuts, and institutional reform. Formed from the Brexit Party in 2018.

Key priorities

  • Immigration reduction
  • Tax cuts and deregulation
  • NHS reform
  • House of Lords abolition
  • Energy independence

Scotland

SNP

Leader: John Swinney

Position: Centre-left

Founded: 1934

A centre-left party seeking Scottish independence. The largest party in Scotland since 2007, with a focus on health, education, and social welfare.

Key priorities

  • Scottish independence
  • NHS and social care funding
  • Child poverty reduction
  • Free tuition and education
  • Renewable energy leadership

Wales

Plaid Cymru

Leader: Rhun ap Iorwerth

Position: Centre-left

Founded: 1925

A centre-left Welsh nationalist party seeking independence and protecting the Welsh language. Strongest in Welsh-speaking north and west Wales.

Key priorities

  • Welsh independence
  • Welsh language preservation
  • Fair funding for Wales
  • Green jobs and energy
  • Affordable housing

Northern Ireland

DUP

Leader: Gavin Robinson

Position: Right-wing

Founded: 1971

A right-wing unionist party committed to Northern Ireland remaining in the UK. Emphasises traditional social values and Euroscepticism.

Key priorities

  • Union with Great Britain
  • Traditional social values
  • Northern Ireland economy
  • Opposition to the Northern Ireland Protocol
  • Strong defence and security

Sinn Féin

Leader: Mary Lou McDonald

Position: Left-wing

Founded: 1905

A left-wing Irish republican party seeking a united Ireland. Follows abstentionism — MPs do not take their Westminster seats.

Key priorities

  • Irish reunification
  • Social housing investment
  • Healthcare reform
  • All-Ireland economy
  • Irish language rights

SDLP

Leader: Colum Eastwood

Position: Centre-left

Founded: 1970

A centre-left Irish nationalist party pursuing a united Ireland through peaceful, constitutional means. Rejects abstentionism.

Key priorities

  • Irish reunification (peaceful)
  • Good Friday Agreement
  • Public services investment
  • Cross-community cooperation
  • Economic development

UUP

Leader: Mike Nesbitt

Position: Centre-right

Founded: 1905

A centre-right unionist party supporting Northern Ireland in the UK. Historically dominant, it backs constitutional politics and power-sharing.

Key priorities

  • Union with Great Britain
  • Power-sharing stability
  • Economic development
  • Health service reform
  • Education investment

Alliance

Leader: Naomi Long

Position: Centrist

Founded: 1970

A centrist cross-community party in Northern Ireland, neither nationalist nor unionist. Supports shared governance and reconciliation.

Key priorities

  • Cross-community reconciliation
  • Education investment
  • Environmental sustainability
  • Progressive social policies
  • Economic reform
