UK Political Parties

An overview of the main political parties across the United Kingdom, including parties from Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

EnglandScotlandWalesNorthern Ireland

England

Labour

Leader: Keir Starmer · Ideology: Centre-left

Focuses on public services, workers' rights, and social justice.

Conservative

Leader: Kemi Badenoch · Ideology: Centre-right

Focuses on lower taxation, free markets, and national sovereignty.

Liberal Democrat

Leader: Ed Davey · Ideology: Centrist

Advocates for civil liberties, electoral reform, and environmentalism.

Green Party

Leader: Carla Denyer & Adrian Ramsay · Ideology: Left-wing

Focuses on climate action, social justice, and grassroots democracy.

ReformUK

Leader: Nigel Farage · Ideology: Right-wing populist

Campaigns for lower immigration, tax cuts, and reform of public institutions.

Scotland

SNP

Leader: John Swinney · Ideology: Centre-left

Campaigns for Scottish independence and progressive domestic policies.

Wales

Plaid Cymru

Leader: Rhun ap Iorwerth · Ideology: Centre-left

Advocates for Welsh independence and the protection of the Welsh language.

Northern Ireland

DUP

Leader: Gavin Robinson · Ideology: Right-wing

A unionist party in Northern Ireland, socially conservative and Eurosceptic.

SinnFéin

Leader: Mary Lou McDonald · Ideology: Left-wing

An Irish republican party advocating for a united Ireland.

SDLP

Leader: Colum Eastwood · Ideology: Centre-left

An Irish nationalist party supporting a united Ireland through peaceful means.

UUP

Leader: Mike Nesbitt · Ideology: Centre-right

A unionist party in Northern Ireland committed to constitutional politics.

Alliance

Leader: Naomi Long · Ideology: Centrist

A cross-community party in Northern Ireland, neither nationalist nor unionist.
